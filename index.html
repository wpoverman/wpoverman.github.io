Bio

My research focuses on developing theoretically grounded approaches to AI safety and alignment [NeurIPS'24, NeurIPS'25], with a particular emphasis on AI control and scalable oversight. I'm especially interested in how tools from black-box uncertainty quantification, reinforcement learning, and theoretical computer science can help us reason rigorously about the behavior of powerful AI systems.

I’m a Ph.D. student in Operations, Information, and Technology at Stanford Graduate School of Business, advised by Mohsen Bayati. Before Stanford, I was a visiting researcher in South Korea and earned an M.Sc. in Computer Science from UC Irvine. I graduated from Caltech in 2020 with a double major in Mathematics and Computer Science.

In addition to my primary focus on AI safety and alignment, my research also explores RL, causal inference, and AI applications in healthcare. Throughout my graduate studies, I have interned at Uber, where I have applied my research work in RL and causal inference to problems in the ridesharing marketplace. During my undergraduate studies at Caltech, I primarily focused on theoretical computer science and extremal combinatorics.
